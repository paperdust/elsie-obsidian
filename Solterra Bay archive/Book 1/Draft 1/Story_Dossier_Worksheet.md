## author's_voice_and_writing_style
### reader_experience_goal

**reader_experience_goal:** Readers should feel the safety of the library and the thrill of the puzzle. The story blends a murder mystery with the comfort of a slice-of-life fantasy. The world should feel ancient and solid, full of stone, books, and quiet corners, creating a sense of "hygge" without focusing on the weather outside.

### overall_story_pov

**narration_style:** Third Person Limited, strictly anchored to Cornelius Drake. The narrative voice acts as a filter of "fussy intellect," interpreting the surroundings through the lens of a fastidious, inventory-obsessed dragon.

**story_type:** A linear mystery investigation mixed with "slice of life" moments. The pacing allows for quiet scenes—drinking tea, mending books, complaining about dust—that are just as important as finding clues.

**pov_approach:** Internal thoughts are crucial. Cornelius's POV should reveal his high-strung nature, his hidden soft spot for his friends, and his need for order.

**deep_pov:** Filter the world through Cornelius's logic and physical senses. Focus on tactile feelings—the texture of paper or the "prickle" of disorder. Do not describe smells unless they are specific clues (like chemicals) or part of a meal being eaten.

### baseline_prose_style

**clarity_and_conciseness:** Prose should be clear and precise to match Cornelius’s voice. Use simple, direct language for action. Ensure the reading level remains accessible (8th grade or lower).

**description_approach:** Emphasize the texture of the world: stone, paper, ink, and tools. Do not mention sunlight or the weather. Focus on concrete details. Only mention smells if they are vital clues or involve food being served.

**dialogue_style:** High contrast drives the humor. Cornelius speaks in complete, formal sentences with no contractions; Zeph uses casual slang.

**sentence_structure_variety:** Mix short sentences for actions with slightly longer ones for Cornelius’s thoughts. Avoid complex or confusing sentence structures.

**verb_choice:** Use active verbs that show order and control (sorted, placed, fixed) for Cornelius.

**pacing:** Move naturally between the mystery and daily life. Do not slow down for long atmospheric descriptions unless they contain clues.

**diversify_vocabulary_and_avoid_predictable_phrasing:** Use specific words for library items (shelf, index, scroll) and the setting (tiles, stone). Avoid overly difficult words; keep the language simple but specific.

**engagement_priority:** Every scene should involve a deduction, a character moment, or an action. Avoid static descriptions of the room or area.

**stylized_language_control:** Cornelius can treat small messes like huge disasters. Use dramatic but simple language for his reactions to disorder.

**style_reference:** Follow Chicago Manual of Style.

**punctuation_preference:** Standard punctuation; use em-dashes for Cornelius's extra thoughts or interruptions.

### core_voice_and_emotion

**voice_authenticity:** Cornelius sounds like a fussy academic. Zeph sounds like a regular teenager. Their voices must stay distinct.

**emotion_rendering:** Show emotion through physical actions. Cornelius might preen his scales when happy or go still when focused. Zeph might fidgit when nervous.

**flaws_and_growth:** Cornelius's need for order is both funny and a problem he must overcome. Zeph grows by becoming more confident. Show this through their actions.

**descriptor_cap:** No descriptor (adjective, phrase, or metaphor) may appear more than three times per manuscript.

### writing_limitations_and_goals

**avoid_clichés:** Avoid dark or gritty fantasy tropes. The villain is a criminal, not a monster.

**vivid_language:** Use strong, simple sensory words (crisp, dusty, silent, savory). Avoid describing light or air.

**emotional_clarity:** Slow down at key emotional moments to make sure the reader feels the connection between characters.

**internal_consistency:** Cornelius must always act like a dragon who loves order. His logic always serves his collection.

**show_don’t_tell:** Show affection through actions, like sharing food or protecting someone, rather than just saying it.

**strong_prose:** Keep the writing tight. Remove extra words that do not add meaning.

### scene_and_chapter_dynamics

**scene_opening:** Open every scene *in media res*—start in the middle of dialogue or action. Do not describe the sunlight, weather, or time of day. Establish the scene through immediate character interaction.

**scene_closure:** End on a clue, a choice, or a realization. Every scene should move the story or character arc forward.

**single_pov_per_scene:** One POV per scene/chapter (Cornelius).

**transition_handling:** Use clear breaks between scenes. Mark changes in location with immediate action in the new setting.

## cozy_fantasy_elements
**mystery_magic_integration:**
Magic in Solterra Bay is not a "Solve-All" button; it is another layer of physics that leaves evidence. The central conflict (The Murder/Theft) is driven by *character* flaws (Greed, Pride), but the *execution* involves fantasy elements (melting locks, species-specific jumping ability).
*   *Rule:* The reader must know the capabilities of the species/magic before they are used to solve the crime.
*   *Application:* We establish Imelda's metal-sensing and Cornelius's scent/taste sensitivity early so they can be used as forensic tools later.

**detective_magical_kit:**
Cornelius Drake is a "Sherlock Holmes" whose superpowers are biological, not deduced.
*   **Omniglot Reading:** He can read the history of the paper/ink itself (dating the letters).
*   **Draconic Senses:** He smells "Ozone" (Magic) vs "Burnt Almond" (Chemical Accelerant).
*   **Hoard Connection:** He physically feels when his library is disordered ("The Prickle").

**magical_clues_and_forensics:**
*   **Ozone Smell:** Indicates a spell was used (or attempted).
*   **Lapis Dust:** Indicates connection to the Blue Glaze/Pottery trade (or a Golem/Construct, but here it's Pottery).
*   **Species Traits:**
    *   *Siren:* Can hold breath (cistern escape).
    *   *Satyr:* High jump (entry via high window).
    *   *Gorgon:* Stun gaze (incapacitating a victim silently).

**cozy_stakes_scaling:**
The fate of the world is **not** at risk. The "Dark Lord" is not coming.
*   **The Stakes:**
    1.  **Sanctuary:** The violation of the Library's peace.
    2.  **Reputation:** The honor of House Kallistos and House Doukas.
    3.  **Financial:** The survival of a local business family.
*   **Why it feels High:** Because to Cornelius, the Library *is* his world. To the town, the Tile Families are their economy.

**the_nature_of_justice (Equilibrium):**
In Cozy Fantasy, the goal is **Restoration**, not Retribution.
*   **The Villain:** Alexandros is not an "Evil Sorcerer"; he is a desperate businessman who made a fatal mistake.
*   **The Punishment:** He is arrested by the proper authorities (Theo) to face trial. There is no vigilante execution.
*   **The Resolution:** The stolen items are returned to their proper place. The community heals. The "Silence" returns.

**fantasy_tropes_subverted:**
*   **The Hoarding Dragon:** Cornelius hoards *public knowledge* and shares it (mostly).
*   **The Chosen One:** Zeph is "Touched" by ancestry, but he's just an intern with a broom, not a warrior with a sword.
*   **The Ancient Curse:** The "Curse" of the Kallistos family is just a messy affair from 40 years ago, de-mythologizing the past.

**atmospheric_magic:**
The setting itself feels alive and magical without overt spell-casting.
*   *The Light:* Exploring how the sun interacts with the "living stone" of the town.
*   *The Verticality:* A town built for flying creatures (Dragons/Sphinxes) vs walking creatures (Humans/Orcs).

## world_building


**overview_setting_summary:** Solterra Bay is a vibrant, vertical city-state inspired by Mediterranean antiquity (Greece/Rome/Phoenicia) but populated by a cosmopolitan mix of Fantasy species. It is a world of "Heat, Light, and Stone," where salt air eats mental, the sun bleaches white walls, and community gossip travels faster than magic. The tone is "Cozy Fantasy"—stakes are emotional and social, not apocalyptic. The town is physically defined by its verticality (Upper Town for prestige, Harbor for commerce) and its primary industry: **Ceramic Tiles**.

### key_world_details

**key_world_detail_1:** **Ecological Dragons:** "Library Dragons" (*Draco bibliotheca*) like Cornelius are a distinct species that spontaneously generate when a collection of books reaches "Critical Knowledge Mass." Their size scales with the size of their library.

**key_world_detail_2:** **Diverse Demographics:** The town is 40% Human, 35% Mythological Kin (Sirens, Satyrs, Gorgons), 12% Elf, and 10% Orc (Phoenic heritage). Species traits (scales, horns, gills) are common and integrated into daily jobs (e.g., Siren fishermen, Gorgon architects).

**key_world_detail_3:** **The Three Tile Families:** Politics are dominated by the rival ceramic houses: **Kallistos** (Tradition/Old Money), **Doukas** (Art/Innovation), and **Veneti** (volume/Export). Their feuds drive the local economy and social friction.

**key_world_detail_4:** **Domestic Magic:** Magic exists but is strictly "Cozy" and utilitarian. It is used for mending pots, cooling ice-boxes, or easing pain. It cannot resurrect the dead, cure old age, or replace hard work.

**key_world_detail_5:** **The Spring Trading Feast:** The annual economic heartbeat of the town. For one week, the population doubles with foreign sailors and merchants. The "Winter Storm Wards" on the harbor are lowered, making the town vulnerable to both trade and crime.

### key_world_rules
[World Rules specific to the Cozy Fantasy Mystery Genre]

**key_world_rule_1:** **Magic is for Mending, not Ending.** There are no fireballs or "Avada Kedavra" spells. Conflict is physical or social; magic deals with the aftermath (healing/repair).

**key_world_rule_2:** **Species Abilities are consistent Clues.** A mystery solution must play fair with biology (e.g., A Siren can hold their breath to escape via a cistern; a Satyr can jump high walls; a Dragon can taste ink age).

**key_world_rule_3:** **Sanctuary is Sacred.** Certain places (The Library, The Temple, The Bathhouse) are neutral ground. Violence in these places is considered a blasphemy against the town itself.

**key_world_rule_4:** **Gossip is Currency.** In a tight-knit town, reputation is everything. Information flows through the Washhouse and the Bakery. If you want to know a secret, you don't spy; you buy a pastry.

**key_world_rule_5:** **The Sea Rules All.** Solterra Bay lives and dies by the tide. Weather and sailing schedules dictate the flow of the plot (and the escape route of the killer).

## plot_development

### plot_beats

**beat_1_hook:** Cornelius Drake enjoys the perfect silence of his library sanctuary, establishing his obsession with order and his "hoard" of books. The impending chaos of the Spring Trading Feast threatens this peace.

**beat_2_inciting_incident:** Cornelius discovers the body of Elias of Tyre in the sealed Kallistos Vault. The shelf for the "Kallistos Letters" is empty. The sanctuary has been violated.

**beat_3_call_to_adventure:** Captain Theo Melas dismisses the murder as natural causes and closes the investigation. Cornelius refuses to accept this disorder and decides to investigate "off the books."

**beat_4_first_major_clue:** Cornelius finds the "Blue Thread" on Sophia Doukas's dress and later sees the same thread on Sister Photine, linking the suspects. He also finds the "Map" on Petros.

**beat_5_midpoint_reversal:** Cornelius publicly accuses Petros Kallistos, only to be proven completely wrong by an ironclad alibi (Petros was in jail). The theory shatters, and the town turns hostile.

**beat_6_deduction_pivot:** Cornelius and Zeph regroup in the library. They realize Elias wasn't a victim but a partner. The motive shifts from "Industrial Espionage" (Glaze) to "Blackmail" (Love Letters).

**beat_7_all_is_lost_crisis:** Alone in the alley, Cornelius is physically threatened by Alexandros Doukas, who smells of "Ozone and Burnt Almond." He realizes the killer is dangerous and physically superior.

**beat_8_epiphany:** Cornelius and Zeph realize the letters were never removed from the library because the Wards would have trapped them. The MacGuffin is still in the building.

**beat_9_climax_showdown:** Cornelius sets a trap in the library. Alexandros returns. Cornelius chooses to save his books from fire rather than just catch the killer, using the environment to pin Alexandros.

**beat_10_resolution:** The "Parlor Room" reveal. Cornelius explains the crime to Theo and the suspects. Alexandros is arrested. Equilibrium is restored to the library.
