## Chapter 1

### **Major Plot Events & Outcomes:**

- Cornelius Drake, a small dragon and meticulous Curator of the Solterra Bay Library, enforces strict “no food” rules when his teenage intern Zephyr (Zeph) brings in a crumbly pastry, establishing their dynamic and Cornelius’s obsession with order.
- The annual Spring Trading Feast begins in Solterra Bay, a week-long event Cornelius dreads for the noise, crowds, and disruption to library routine.
- A nervous courier, Elias of Tyre, arrives with a small, leather-wrapped package to be stored in the Kallistos family’s private vault within the library.
- Cornelius notices suspicious residue on the package (powdered lapis lazuli and fresh machine oil) and Elias’s physical signs of stress, leading him to suspect the declared contents (“sentimental correspondence”) are false.
- Cornelius agrees to store the package but forces Elias to complete a detailed intake form, thereby creating a formal record linking Elias, House Kallistos, and the package.
- After Elias flees, Cornelius decides not to alert the city Watch yet, choosing instead to observe how events unfold.
- Cornelius and Zeph go into the crowded Feast market so Cornelius can buy a specific variety of mint for the library, exposing them to city gossip.
- An herb vendor mentions a rumor about a body or break-in in the Kallistos vault, which Cornelius firmly denies, but the rumor signals that something about the vault is already a subject of public speculation.

- Cornelius interprets the conversation as evidence of a planned attempt to breach the library’s wards (likely targeting the Kallistos vault) and identifies Sophia as a probable instigator, with Elias as her operative.
- They return to the library; the vault transfer drawer and the suspicious package remain undisturbed.
- Cornelius documents the day’s events in a private incident log, formally noting his suspicions about a coordinated effort to break the vault protections.
- On the main floor, Zeph has successfully completed his tasks; Cornelius begrudgingly praises him, signaling incremental trust.
- While examining the Kallistos vault door, Cornelius experiences a deep, instinctual “hoard-threat” prickle, indicating a serious but as-yet unseen danger to the library and its contents.

## Chapter 2

### **Major Plot Events & Outcomes:**

- At dawn, Cornelius opens the Solterra Bay Library and immediately detects an acrid ozone smell indicating recent powerful magic.
- Tracing the smell to the Kallistos family vault, he finds the iron door’s seal melted and still faintly warm, showing a very recent magical breach.
- Inside the vault:
  - The shelf containing Kallistos “correspondence” is empty—those documents have been stolen.
  - The ancient protective wards have clearly been bypassed or neutralized.
  - The body of Elias of Tyre (the courier from the previous day) lies dead on the floor with no visible injuries.
- Cornelius deduces from the burnt-almond scent (cyanide-like), especially around Elias’s mouth and nose, that Elias was killed by an inhaled poison, not physical violence.
- He notes a second set of scuff marks in the dust near the door indicating at least one other, heavier person was present.
- Zeph arrives; Cornelius informs him of the theft and death, then sends him to quietly sweep the building for intruders while preserving the crime scene.
- Investigating the vault further, Cornelius finds soot near the doorframe and the smell of machine oil, linking the breach to the mysterious, oil-scented package delivered by Elias in the previous chapter.
- Checking the transfer drawer, Cornelius discovers the package is gone—it was a tool or key used in the theft, not mere sentimental letters.
- Zeph calls Cornelius to the southern archive, where:
  - A ventilation grate has been removed; fresh boot scuffs indicate recent use of the vents as a covert access route.
  - Petros Kallistos is found slumped drunk or unconscious behind a shelf, with an empty wine bottle and a detailed schematic of the library’s ventilation system in his lap.
- Once roused, Petros admits he was drinking at a tavern the previous night and spoke at length about the vault and its wards to a large, eastern-accented man who bought him drinks; he doesn’t remember how he ended up in the archive or how he got the schematic.
- Cornelius concludes Petros was manipulated for information but is not the killer. He orders Zeph to keep Petros from leaving while he goes to summon the Watch.
- At the front entrance, Cornelius sees Captain Mago Hanno, a notorious orc smuggler, standing across the square and watching the library intently; they lock eyes.
- Disturbed, Cornelius retreats, locks the door, and recognizes that a professional, premeditated theft and murder have already taken place under his nose.

## Chapter 3

### **Major Plot Events & Outcomes:**

- Zeph brings Captain Theo Melas and two Watch guards to the library to investigate the vault death.
- Cornelius presents his initial forensic observations: melted vault seal, missing package, missing Kallistos correspondence, poison scent, and the dead courier Elias.
- Theo immediately asserts Watch jurisdiction, telling Cornelius to stay out of the way and downplaying the evidence as potentially accidental.
- Cornelius walks Theo through the physical evidence on the vault door and explains his suspicion that the delivered “correspondence” package was actually a magical tool used to breach the wards.
- Theo is openly skeptical, suggesting Elias may have been attempting a robbery that went wrong, and insists on a healer’s certification before treating it as murder.
- Sister Photine arrives, conducts a methodical examination of Elias’s body, and notes:
  - Dilated pupils
  - Faint almond scent
  - Fresh rope burns on Elias’s hands
  - Absence of poison-related skin discoloration
- Despite acknowledging some of the signs, Photine rules the death as “natural causes” (likely stress-induced heart failure), explicitly rejecting poison as the primary explanation.
- Cornelius strongly protests, reiterating the cyanide-like markers and tying them to the vault breach and missing items, but Theo accepts Photine’s ruling.
- Theo orders the vault sealed as a crime scene and demands Cornelius surrender the vault key.
- After a tense standoff, Cornelius complies, formally noting he is surrendering the key under protest and formally requesting a full investigation into both the theft and Elias’s death.
- The Watch chains and locks the vault; Cornelius is effectively barred from further official access.
- Cornelius resumes outwardly normal library operations but privately records a detailed incident log in his own ledger, concluding the official investigation is compromised or incompetent and deciding to proceed with his own independent inquiry.
