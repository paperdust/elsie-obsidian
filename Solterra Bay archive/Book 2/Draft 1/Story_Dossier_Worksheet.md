## author's_voice_and_writing_style
### reader_experience_goal

**reader_experience_goal:** Readers should feel the safety of the library and the thrill of the puzzle. The story blends a murder mystery with the comfort of a slice-of-life fantasy. The world should feel ancient and solid, full of stone, books, and quiet corners, creating a sense of "hygge" without focusing on the weather outside.

### overall_story_pov

**narration_style:** Third Person Limited, strictly anchored to Cornelius Drake. The narrative voice acts as a filter of "fussy intellect," interpreting the surroundings through the lens of a fastidious, inventory-obsessed dragon.

**story_type:** A linear mystery investigation mixed with "slice of life" moments. The pacing allows for quiet scenes—drinking tea, mending books, complaining about dust—that are just as important as finding clues.

**pov_approach:** Internal thoughts are crucial. Cornelius's POV should reveal his high-strung nature, his hidden soft spot for his friends, and his need for order.

**deep_pov:** Filter the world through Cornelius's logic and physical senses. Focus on tactile feelings—the texture of paper or the "prickle" of disorder. Do not describe smells unless they are specific clues (like chemicals) or part of a meal being eaten.

### baseline_prose_style

**clarity_and_conciseness:** Prose should be clear and precise to match Cornelius’s voice. Use simple, direct language for action. Ensure the reading level remains accessible (8th grade or lower).

**description_approach:** Emphasize the texture of the world: stone, paper, ink, and tools. Do not mention sunlight or the weather. Focus on concrete details. Only mention smells if they are vital clues or involve food being served.

**dialogue_style:** High contrast drives the humor. Cornelius speaks in complete, formal sentences with no contractions; Zeph uses casual slang.

**sentence_structure_variety:** Mix short sentences for actions with slightly longer ones for Cornelius’s thoughts. Avoid complex or confusing sentence structures.

**verb_choice:** Use active verbs that show order and control (sorted, placed, fixed) for Cornelius.

**pacing:** Move naturally between the mystery and daily life. Do not slow down for long atmospheric descriptions unless they contain clues.

**diversify_vocabulary_and_avoid_predictable_phrasing:** Use specific words for library items (shelf, index, scroll) and the setting (tiles, stone). Avoid overly difficult words; keep the language simple but specific.

**engagement_priority:** Every scene should involve a deduction, a character moment, or an action. Avoid static descriptions of the room or area.

**stylized_language_control:** Cornelius can treat small messes like huge disasters. Use dramatic but simple language for his reactions to disorder.

**style_reference:** Follow Chicago Manual of Style.

**punctuation_preference:** Standard punctuation; use em-dashes for Cornelius's extra thoughts or interruptions.

### core_voice_and_emotion

**voice_authenticity:** Cornelius sounds like a fussy academic. Zeph sounds like a regular teenager. Their voices must stay distinct.

**emotion_rendering:** Show emotion through physical actions. Cornelius might preen his scales when happy or go still when focused. Zeph might fidgit when nervous.

**flaws_and_growth:** Cornelius's need for order is both funny and a problem he must overcome. Zeph grows by becoming more confident. Show this through their actions.

**descriptor_cap:** No descriptor (adjective, phrase, or metaphor) may appear more than three times per manuscript.

### writing_limitations_and_goals

**avoid_clichés:** Avoid dark or gritty fantasy tropes. The villain is a criminal, not a monster.

**vivid_language:** Use strong, simple sensory words (crisp, dusty, silent, savory). Avoid describing light or air.

**emotional_clarity:** Slow down at key emotional moments to make sure the reader feels the connection between characters.

**internal_consistency:** Cornelius must always act like a dragon who loves order. His logic always serves his collection.

**show_don’t_tell:** Show affection through actions, like sharing food or protecting someone, rather than just saying it.

**strong_prose:** Keep the writing tight. Remove extra words that do not add meaning.

### scene_and_chapter_dynamics

**scene_opening:** Open every scene *in media res*—start in the middle of dialogue or action. Do not describe the sunlight, weather, or time of day. Establish the scene through immediate character interaction.

**scene_closure:** End on a clue, a choice, or a realization. Every scene should move the story or character arc forward.

**single_pov_per_scene:** One POV per scene/chapter (Cornelius).

**transition_handling:** Use clear breaks between scenes. Mark changes in location with immediate action in the new setting.

## cozy_fantasy_elements
**cozy_atmosphere:** Solterra Bay is designed for comfort. The primary conflicts are social (gossip) or professional (rivalries), not existential.
*   **The Vibe:** Sun-drenched stone, cool marble, and the smell of baking bread. Even the "Crime Scene" is a luxury guest house.
*   **Comfort Rituals:** Tea (Mint for Cornelius), slow-roasted chickpeas, communally shared meals, and the "Afternoon Nap" (Siesta) which is sacred.

**fantasy_integration:** Magic is utilitarian and domestic, woven into the background.
*   **Domestic Magic:** Cooling boxes keep fish fresh; pest wards keep ants out of sugar; mending spells fix broken teacups.
*   **Species Integration:** Mythological kin (Satyrs, Orcs, Sirens) hold regular jobs. A Satyr isn't a forest spirit; he's the baker who complains about flour prices.
*   **The Protagonist:** Cornelius is a Dragon, but "Dragon-ness" implies hoarding books and being grumpy, not burning villages.

**mystery_mechanics:** The mystery follows strict "Fair Play" rules adapted for fantasy.
*   **Species Traits as Clues:** A clue might be "The thief jumped 10 feet vertically" (pointing to a Satyr) or "The thief held their breath for 10 minutes" (Siren).
*   **Magic Limitation:** Magic cannot solve the crime instantly (no "Truth Spells" allowed in court). Magic leaves traces (Residue) that can be analyzed forensically.
*   **Cornelius's Edge:** His non-human senses (smell/heat detection) allow him to find clues humans miss, essentially acting as a "CSI Lab" on legs.

**low_stakes_physics:** The world is safe for the reader.
*   **Non-Lethal:** Murder is rare; theft/vandalism is preferred. If death occurs, it is bloodless (poison/magic).
*   **Restorative Justice:** The goal is to restore order, not punish. Brother Demos cleans floors as penance; Kyriakos is arrested, not killed.
*   **Community Core:** The town rallies to protect its own. Gossip is a defense mechanism.

**sensory_details:** The narrative prioritizes pleasant sensory experiences even during tension.
*   **Visuals:** Bioluminescent beetles, colored glass, sparkling ocean.
*   **Taste/Smell:** Lemon, oregano, brine, beeswax. (Note: Smell emphasis dialed back per instruction).
*   **Sound:** The distant roar of the ocean vs. the quiet of the library.

**romantic_elements:** (Subplot Only)
*   **Type:** Sweet/Awkward (Zeph & Sophia).
*   **Role:** Adds warmth and stakes (Zeph wants to impress her) without overshadowing the mystery.


## world_building
**overview_setting_summary:** Solterra Bay is a vertical, sun-drenched coastal town built into limestone cliffs. It is a cosmopolitan mix of Humans (40%), Mythological Kin (35% - Satyrs/Sirens/Gorgons), and Elder Races (Elves/Orcs) living in "Cozy Equilibrium." The aesthetic is Mediterranean Antiquity (stone, terracotta, aqueducts) mixed with domestic magic.

### key_world_details

**key_world_detail_1:** **Demographics:** Integration is total. Mythological Kin (Satyrs/Sirens/Gorgons) hold regular jobs—Satyrs are bakers, Orcs are merchants.

**key_world_detail_2:** **Magic System:** "Domestic Magic." Strictly functional: cooling boxes for food, pest wards for pantries, and mending charms for repairs. No combat magic (fireballs/lightning).

**key_world_detail_3:** **The Tile Families:** Three rival guilds determine the town's aesthetic and economy: **Kallistos** (Traditional/Heavy), **Doukas** (Artistic/The Innovators), and **Veneti** (Merchant/Export).

**key_world_detail_4:** **Dragon Ecology:** Library Dragons (*Draco bibliotheca*) are cat-sized guardians who bond to specific buildings. They hoard knowledge and organized collections, not gold.

**key_world_detail_5:** **Twinkle Nights (The Event):** A bioluminescent beetle migration. The town enforces a strict "Lights Out" policy for three nights, creating a unique visual landscape of deep shadows and blue-green insect glow.

### key_world_rules

**key_world_rule_1:** **The Cozy Physics:** Violence is non-graphic and rare. Conflicts are resolved through social pressure, specific skills (Botany/Ceramics), or non-lethal confrontation.

**key_world_rule_2:** **The Fishbowl Effect:** Privacy is impossible due to the dense, vertical architecture. Neighbors overhear conversations through open windows; gossip spreads instantly.

**key_world_rule_3:** **Mending over Breaking:** The culture values repair (Kintsugi/Healing) over destruction. Broken objects are fixed with gold or magic; relationships are mended rather than severed.

**key_world_rule_4:** **Technological Level:** Antiquity/Medieval mix. Beeswax candles and olive oil lamps provide light (no electricity/gas). Aqueducts provide running water.

**key_world_rule_5:** **Fair Play Biology:** Mystery solutions often hinge on species traits (e.g., a Siren holding their breath, a Satyr's jumping ability) rather than magic.

**beat_17_epiphany_ch17:** **The Plan:** On the switchback, Cornelius realizes he can't wrestle the jar (it will fall/break). He asks about the Lead Seal. He decides to use his fire to *weld* the jar shut, trapping the evidence inside.

**beat_18_showdown_ch18:** **The Climax:** Zeph distracts Kyriakos with flattery ("Nice Jar!"). Cornelius welds the jar. Kyriakos is defeated by his own tool. The Orchid is saved.

**beat_19_resolution_ch19:** **The Arrest:** Captain Theo arrests Kyriakos (Poaching/Theft). Zeph finds the Journal. Sophia decides to stay in town.

**beat_20_full_circle_ch20:** **The Return:** The Journal is returned. Demos is cleaning floors (Penance). Cornelius places the Welded Jar on his shelf. The "Cozy" world is restored.
