## author's_voice_and_writing_style

[**NOTE TO AUTHORS:** This section is intended to set a baseline for good prose output.]

### reader_experience_goal

**reader_experience_goal:** [Readers should feel immersed in a charming, slightly whimsical, but logically grounded magical world. The narrative should blend the intellectual satisfaction of a fair-play mystery with the emotional warmth of a found-family fantasy. The pacing should be leisurely but steady, emphasizing atmosphere and character over adrenaline. Think *Legends and Lattes* meets *Sherlock Holmes*.]

### overall_story_pov

**narration_style:** [Third Person Limited (Cornelius Drake). The voice should filter everything through his academic, draconic, and slightly haughty perspective. He notices scents, temperatures, and structural integrity.]

**story_type:** [Linear Cozy Mystery. The structure is the 4-Act Grid.]

**pov_approach:** [Cornelius is a Dragon in a human-sized world. He is huge, precise, and careful not to break things. He is biased against "inefficiency" and "loud noises".]

**deep_pov:** [Filter through sensory experience, especially smell (chemical analysis) and heat. Maintain a tone of "long-suffering intellect" that softens into genuine care for his hoard (the town).]

## world_building

**overview_setting_summary:** [Solterra Bay is a coastal town built into white limestone cliffs. It is famous for its magical flora. The Library of Solterra is carved into the rock. The vibe is "Mediterranean Magic."]

### key_world_details
**key_world_detail_1:** [**The Library:** A sanctuary of knowledge, guarded by Cornelius. It has strict rules. It is an "Attractor" of quiet.]
**key_world_detail_2:** [**Twinkle Nights:** A bioluminescent firefly migration that happens annually. The town goes dark to celebrate. This provides the "Ticking Clock".]
**key_world_detail_3:** [**Dragon Senses:** Cornelius can smell emotions (chemically) and heat/stress.]

### key_world_rules
**key_world_rule_1:** [**Lethality is Zero.** No one dies. Fights are slapstick or restrained.]
**key_world_rule_2:** [**Magic has limits.** It's mostly craft-based (growing plants, preserving items).]
**key_world_rule_3:** [**Gossip Travels Fast.** The "Fishbowl Effect". Everyone knows everyone's business.]

## act_1 (Chapters 1-5)
**act_purpose:** [Establish the Equilibrium (Library), introduce the Inciting Incident (Theft), and launch the Sleeuth on the False Path (Suspect Demos).]

### chapter_1
**chapter_summary_for_writer:** [Cornelius is performing a routine inventory in the Solterra Bay Library, enjoying the gloom while the chaotic "Twinkle Nights" festival roars outside. He discovers a discrepancy: *Secret Wonders: Field notes on medicinal herbs* is missing from the shelf. He asks his assistant, Zeph, about it, but Zeph is too distracted by his reflection and his upcoming date with Sophia Doukas to care. Cornelius, unwilling to let the Town Watch inside during the festival, decides to mark it "Misplaced" and fix it tomorrow. The chapter ends with a frantic, un-library-like banging on the main door.]
**emotional_shift:** [From Satisfaction (Counting) to Annoyance (Discrepancy).]
**key_information_and_world_building_revealed:** [Cornelius is a Dragon and a Librarian. He hoards "Order". The Festival is happening. Zeph has a crush.]

### chapter_2
**chapter_summary_for_writer:** [Dr. Anthea Floros bursts in, claiming her *Botanist's Journal* (containing the location of the Moon-Scented Orchid) has been stolen from the Sea View Guest House. Cornelius is skeptical, noting her disheveled state and muddy hem. He smells "Synthetic Musk" on her but checks the window. He sees Brother Demos sprinting away down the alley, clutching a leather-bound book that perfectly matches the missing volume *and* the journal's description. The logic snaps into place: Demos is the thief. Cornelius accepts the case, motivated by the intellectual puzzle and the desire to punish a book thief.]
**emotional_shift:** [From Skepticism to Predatory Focus (The Hunt).]
**key_information_and_world_building_revealed:** [The Crime (Journal Theft). The Suspect (Demos). The Clue (Synthetic Musk - overlooked).]

### chapter_3
**chapter_summary_for_writer:** [Cornelius and Zeph investigate the crime scene (Guest House Room). It is a mess of botany. They find a dust outline where the book was and crushed glass shards on the floor. Zeph identifies the scent as "Kyriakos No. 5" (cheap perfume) and notes the glass is "Borosilicate" (Lab Glass). They spot Sophia Doukas sketching on the terrace below, distracting Zeph. Cornelius dismisses the perfume/glass as "messy habits" and confirms the window was unlatched. He is convinced Demos is the culprit and drags Zeph away to hunt the monk.]
**emotional_shift:** [Validation (Clues found) / Zeph's distraction.]
**key_information_and_world_building_revealed:** [New Clues: Borosilicate Glass, Kyriakos No. 5. Sophia is introduced.]

### chapter_4
**chapter_summary_for_writer:** [Cornelius goes to the Docks to interview Nephele (Fishmonger), who was seen near the Guest House. The sensory environment is overwhelming (fish guts). Nephele admits she was there delivering oysters to Kyriakos Vlahos (The Perfumer). She denies the theft but mentions seeing a "hooded figure" (Demos). Cornelius clears her (she is too loud/clumsy for a stealth theft) but flags Kyriakos's presence as odd. He returns to the library, sure that Demos is the only viable suspect.]
**emotional_shift:** [Disgust (Fish) to Satisfaction (Suspect cleared, theory verified).]
**key_information_and_world_building_revealed:** [Kyriakos is in town. Nephele is innocent.]
