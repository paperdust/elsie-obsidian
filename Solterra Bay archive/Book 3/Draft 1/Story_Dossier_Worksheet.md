## author's_voice_and_writing_style
### reader_experience_goal

**reader_experience_goal:** Readers should feel the safety of the library and the thrill of the puzzle. The story blends a murder mystery with the comfort of a slice-of-life fantasy. The world should feel ancient and solid, full of stone, books, and quiet corners, creating a sense of "hygge" without focusing on the weather outside.

### overall_story_pov

**narration_style:** Third Person Limited, strictly anchored to Cornelius Drake. The narrative voice acts as a filter of "fussy intellect," interpreting the surroundings through the lens of a fastidious, inventory-obsessed dragon.

**story_type:** A linear mystery investigation mixed with "slice of life" moments. The pacing allows for quiet scenes—drinking tea, mending books, complaining about dust—that are just as important as finding clues.

**pov_approach:** Internal thoughts are crucial. Cornelius's POV should reveal his high-strung nature, his hidden soft spot for his friends, and his need for order.

**deep_pov:** Filter the world through Cornelius's logic and physical senses. Focus on tactile feelings—the texture of paper or the "prickle" of disorder. Do not describe smells unless they are specific clues (like chemicals) or part of a meal being eaten.

### baseline_prose_style

**clarity_and_conciseness:** Prose should be clear and precise to match Cornelius’s voice. Use simple, direct language for action. Ensure the reading level remains accessible (8th grade or lower).

**description_approach:** Emphasize the texture of the world: stone, paper, ink, and tools. Do not mention sunlight or the weather. Focus on concrete details. Only mention smells if they are vital clues or involve food being served.

**dialogue_style:** High contrast drives the humor. Cornelius speaks in complete, formal sentences with no contractions; Zeph uses casual slang.

**sentence_structure_variety:** Mix short sentences for actions with slightly longer ones for Cornelius’s thoughts. Avoid complex or confusing sentence structures.

**verb_choice:** Use active verbs that show order and control (sorted, placed, fixed) for Cornelius.

**pacing:** Move naturally between the mystery and daily life. Do not slow down for long atmospheric descriptions unless they contain clues.

**diversify_vocabulary_and_avoid_predictable_phrasing:** Use specific words for library items (shelf, index, scroll) and the setting (tiles, stone). Avoid overly difficult words; keep the language simple but specific.

**engagement_priority:** Every scene should involve a deduction, a character moment, or an action. Avoid static descriptions of the room or area.

**stylized_language_control:** Cornelius can treat small messes like huge disasters. Use dramatic but simple language for his reactions to disorder.

**style_reference:** Follow Chicago Manual of Style.

**punctuation_preference:** Standard punctuation; use em-dashes for Cornelius's extra thoughts or interruptions.

### core_voice_and_emotion

**voice_authenticity:** Cornelius sounds like a fussy academic. Zeph sounds like a regular teenager. Their voices must stay distinct.

**emotion_rendering:** Show emotion through physical actions. Cornelius might preen his scales when happy or go still when focused. Zeph might fidgit when nervous.

**flaws_and_growth:** Cornelius's need for order is both funny and a problem he must overcome. Zeph grows by becoming more confident. Show this through their actions.

**descriptor_cap:** No descriptor (adjective, phrase, or metaphor) may appear more than three times per manuscript.

### writing_limitations_and_goals

**avoid_clichés:** Avoid dark or gritty fantasy tropes. The villain is a criminal, not a monster.

**vivid_language:** Use strong, simple sensory words (crisp, dusty, silent, savory). Avoid describing light or air.

**emotional_clarity:** Slow down at key emotional moments to make sure the reader feels the connection between characters.

**internal_consistency:** Cornelius must always act like a dragon who loves order. His logic always serves his collection.

**show_don’t_tell:** Show affection through actions, like sharing food or protecting someone, rather than just saying it.

**strong_prose:** Keep the writing tight. Remove extra words that do not add meaning.

### scene_and_chapter_dynamics

**scene_opening:** Open every scene *in media res*—start in the middle of dialogue or action. Do not describe the sunlight, weather, or time of day. Establish the scene through immediate character interaction.

**scene_closure:** End on a clue, a choice, or a realization. Every scene should move the story or character arc forward.

**single_pov_per_scene:** One POV per scene/chapter (Cornelius).

**transition_handling:** Use clear breaks between scenes. Mark changes in location with immediate action in the new setting.

## world_building
### overview_setting_summary
**overview_setting_summary:** Solterra Bay is a vertical, sun-drenched coastal town built of white stone and terracotta. It is a place of "Scarcity turned to Art," defined by the smell of salt, hot stone, and old paper. The population is a cosmopolitan mix of Humans, Mythological Kin (Sirens, Satyrs, Sphinxes), Elves, and Orcs, all integrated into a bustling merchant society dominated by the rival Tile Families.

### key_world_details

**key_world_detail_1:** **The Tile Families:** Three rival houses (Kallistos, Doukas, Veneti) control the economy and politics. Their feuds regarding tradition vs. innovation drive the social tension.

**key_world_detail_2:** **Mythological Coexistence:** "Kin" species (Sirens, Gorgons, Satyrs) are not hidden/magical creatures but everyday citizens with specific roles (e.g., Gorgons as masons, Sirens as fishermen).

**key_world_detail_3:** **Draconic Ecology:** Library Dragons like Cornelius are not born biologically but "manifest" from a Critical Mass of Knowledge. They bond to the library first, the librarian second.

**key_world_detail_4:** **The Sensory Signature:** The town is defined by *Heat* (Sunlight), *Stone* (Architecture), and *Salt* (Sea). It is an "Illuminated" world where secrets are hard to hide.

**key_world_detail_5:** **Technology Level:** Antiquity/Early Medieval. No electricity; lighting is oil/beeswax. Advanced aqueducts. Communication via Centaur Couriers or Ships.

### key_world_rules

**key_world_rule_1:** **Lethality is Low/Non-Graphic:** Violence is physical but rarely deadly; the focus is on social/political conflict. Magic heals but cannot resurrect.

**key_world_rule_2:** **Magic is Domestic:** Magic is used for mending pots, heating tea, or cleaning; combat magic (fireballs) is nonexistent.

**key_world_rule_3:** **The Gossip Field:** Information travels instantly via an ambient "Gossip Network." If two people know a secret, the whole town knows by noon.

**key_world_rule_4:** **Species Abilities are Mundane:** A Siren's voice calms storms/brawls; a Gorgon's gaze stuns for safety. Abilities are integrated into labor, not war.

**key_world_rule_5:** **The Episodic Invariant:** The town must return to a recognizable status quo (Order) by the end of the mystery.

## cozy_fantasy_elements
### cozy_fantasy_mystery_elements

**genre_centrality:**
*   **Logic:** The fantasy elements are not for war or epic quests, but for "Domestic Convenience" and "Mystery Solving." Magic is a tool for order (Cornelius) or intuition (Zeph), not destruction. The "Cozy" aspect demands low lethality and high warmth.

**magical_laws_and_crimes:**
*   **Forensic Vision:** Cornelius (Library Dragon) can see the age of ink, the weave of paper, and the chemical composition of tea. *Constraint:* He cannot see "intent," only physical facts.
*   **Guardian Sense:** Zephyr (Lamassu) suffers migraines when "Danger" or "Malice" is near. *Constraint:* It is imprecise (a "blunt instrument") and debilitating.
*   **Truth-Sense:** Valerian (Sphinx-kin) sneezes when he hears a lie. *Constraint:* He sneezes at *any* untruth, including polite social lies, making him a social pariah.

**fantasy_species_roles:**
*   **Library Dragons:** Cat-sized, hoard books/knowledge, breathe warming fire (for tea/grates), obsessed with Order.
*   **Lamassu:** Protectors, empathetic, prone to sensory overload in chaotic environments (like Galas).
*   **Sirens:** Their voice can influence emotion, but in a "Cozy" setting, it's used for conflict resolution or art, not drowning sailors. (Chloe uses it to "defuse" tension, or in this case, to mask her crime).
*   **Sphinx-kin:** Obsessed with Riddles and Truth. Cannot lie without physical pain.

**cozy_tropes_integration:**
*   **The Closed Circle:** A magical storm or "Lockdown Spell" isn't needed; the social pressure of the Gala and the Watch Captain's orders keep everyone in City Hall.
*   **The Amateur Sleuth:** Cornelius uses his "Outsider Perspective" (Non-human logic) to solve what the human guards miss.
*   **Domestic Magic:** Imelda uses magic to tidy rooms, organize papers, and brew perfect tea. This sets the "Cozy" atmosphere—magic serves the home.

**mystery_stakes_and_magic:**
*   **The Weapon:** "Judgement Salt." A magical alchemical substance that only kills the *guilty*.
*   **The Twist:** The victim (Lucchesi) died not because he was the *target*, but because he was *guilty* (of theft). The magic worked correctly; the killer's aim was off.
*   **Safety vs Justice:** The town relies on "Order" (Magic/Law), but the resolution depends on "Community" (Messy feelings).

**signature_magic_scenes:**
*   **The Tea Test:** Cornelius heating the tea with a precise breath to activate the antidote/reveal the poison.
*   **The Migraine Radar:** Zeph navigating the crowd, wincing as he gets closer to the killer, guiding Cornelius like a Geiger counter.
*   **The Hoard:** Cornelius retreating to his "Hoard" (The Library) to think, physically curling around the evidence.

**setting_atmosphere (Solterra):**
*   **Sensory Signature:** Heat, bright sunlight, white stone, smell of salt and old paper. A place of "Illumination" where secrets are hard to hide.
*   **Magical Texture:** The air hums with the "Gossip Field" (Information travels instantly via magic/social osmosis).
