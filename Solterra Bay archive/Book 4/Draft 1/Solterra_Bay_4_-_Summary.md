## Chapter 1

### **Major Plot Events & Outcomes:**

- Librarian-dragon Cornelius hides select precious books around the library to avoid including them in an upcoming charity sale.
- Imelda Kassiani confronts him, reiterates that the Temple needs funds for medical supplies, and demands a list of fifty books to donate by sunset.
- Cornelius initially resists, claiming the irreplaceable value of the collection and framing the sale as permanent loss for only temporary relief.
- Noise from the town square draws Cornelius from his work; he flies down to observe an argument at the fountain between Gorgon leatherworker Xanthe and Temple healer Brother Demos.
- Xanthe accuses the Temple of discriminatory neglect after Demos refuses to come treat her cousin’s worsening fever at home.
- Demos explains they must triage due to limited supplies, prioritizing immediate life-or-death cases; Xanthe denounces this as selective cruelty and storms off.
- As the crowd disperses, Cornelius overhears townspeople discussing rumors that Temple donations from major families have dwindled and supplies are severely depleted.
- Cornelius realizes Demos’s refusal stems from scarcity rather than bias, reframing the charity sale as genuinely necessary stopgap aid.
- Returning to the library, Cornelius begins adding more valuable books—ones he had hidden or planned to keep forever—to the donation stack.
- By sunset he assembles forty-seven volumes, falling short of Imelda’s target but far exceeding his initial willingness to part with only three.
- He delivers the list and avoids further discussion; the library closes and he completes his evening routines mechanically.
- Alone on his roost, Cornelius wrestles with whether preserving knowledge matters when people lack basic medical care, falling asleep troubled by the unresolved moral dilemma.

## Chapter 2

### **Major Plot Events & Outcomes:**

- Cornelius is summoned to the Temple Archives and finds Brother Demos fully petrified into stone, mid-task, but still faintly biologically active.
- Cornelius conducts an initial examination of the statue and determines:
  - The petrification is incomplete; Demos is technically still alive.
  - There is an approximate 72-hour window from the moment of petrification in which reversal is possible.
- Based on Imelda’s timeline, Cornelius estimates they have roughly 58–70 hours remaining.
- Cornelius discovers a faint, non-Temple scent of sandalwood near Demos’s stone body, strongest by his bag/left side.
- Captain Theo and two Watch guards arrive; Theo immediately attributes the crime to an unspecified Gorgon and wants to act on that assumption.
- Cornelius argues that:
  - The priority must be restoring Demos, not punishing someone.
  - Reversal requires the original Gorgon’s cooperation, so indiscriminate arrests would be dangerous and counterproductive.
- Sister Photine arrives, reacts with shock and terror, and demands that Theo lock down the Gorgon quarter, indicating strong pre-existing fear and bias.
- After a contentious debate, Theo reluctantly agrees to investigate first rather than immediately locking down or mass-arresting Gorgons, but:
  - He posts guards at the Archive and Temple perimeter.
  - He demands a list of all Gorgons who have had access to the Temple in the last week.
- Cornelius volunteers to compile Gorgon-access data from visitor logs and patient records and to investigate Demos’s movements and schedule to narrow down the petrification time.
- Photine, distraught and feeling guilty, reveals she had told Demos to go home the previous night; she demands that Theo and Cornelius find whoever did this, hinting she has “lost another” before.
- Cornelius concludes this was likely a premeditated attack designed to look accidental and suspects a larger pattern that Photine already recognizes.
- The chapter ends with Cornelius leaving to examine administrative records, convinced that Demos, the Archives, and the sandalwood are all deliberate choices, and that someone wants Gorgons blamed.
