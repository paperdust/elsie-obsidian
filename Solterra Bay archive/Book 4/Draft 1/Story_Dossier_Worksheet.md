## author's_voice_and_writing_style
### reader_experience_goal

**reader_experience_goal:** Readers should feel the safety of the library and the thrill of the puzzle. The story blends a murder mystery with the comfort of a slice-of-life fantasy. The world should feel ancient and solid, full of stone, books, and quiet corners, creating a sense of "hygge" without focusing on the weather outside.

### overall_story_pov

**narration_style:** Third Person Limited, strictly anchored to Cornelius Drake. The narrative voice acts as a filter of "fussy intellect," interpreting the surroundings through the lens of a fastidious, inventory-obsessed dragon.

**story_type:** A linear mystery investigation mixed with "slice of life" moments. The pacing allows for quiet scenes—drinking tea, mending books, complaining about dust—that are just as important as finding clues.

**pov_approach:** Internal thoughts are crucial. Cornelius's POV should reveal his high-strung nature, his hidden soft spot for his friends, and his need for order.

**deep_pov:** Filter the world through Cornelius's logic and physical senses. Focus on tactile feelings—the texture of paper or the "prickle" of disorder. Do not describe smells unless they are specific clues (like chemicals) or part of a meal being eaten.

### baseline_prose_style

**clarity_and_conciseness:** Prose should be clear and precise to match Cornelius’s voice. Use simple, direct language for action. Ensure the reading level remains accessible (8th grade or lower).

**description_approach:** Emphasize the texture of the world: stone, paper, ink, and tools. Do not mention sunlight or the weather. Focus on concrete details. Only mention smells if they are vital clues or involve food being served.

**dialogue_style:** High contrast drives the humor. Cornelius speaks in complete, formal sentences with no contractions; Zeph uses casual slang.

**sentence_structure_variety:** Mix short sentences for actions with slightly longer ones for Cornelius’s thoughts. Avoid complex or confusing sentence structures.

**verb_choice:** Use active verbs that show order and control (sorted, placed, fixed) for Cornelius.

**pacing:** Move naturally between the mystery and daily life. Do not slow down for long atmospheric descriptions unless they contain clues.

**diversify_vocabulary_and_avoid_predictable_phrasing:** Use specific words for library items (shelf, index, scroll) and the setting (tiles, stone). Avoid overly difficult words; keep the language simple but specific.

**engagement_priority:** Every scene should involve a deduction, a character moment, or an action. Avoid static descriptions of the room or area.

**stylized_language_control:** Cornelius can treat small messes like huge disasters. Use dramatic but simple language for his reactions to disorder.

**style_reference:** Follow Chicago Manual of Style.

**punctuation_preference:** Standard punctuation; use em-dashes for Cornelius's extra thoughts or interruptions.

### core_voice_and_emotion

**voice_authenticity:** Cornelius sounds like a fussy academic. Zeph sounds like a regular teenager. Their voices must stay distinct.

**emotion_rendering:** Show emotion through physical actions. Cornelius might preen his scales when happy or go still when focused. Zeph might fidgit when nervous.

**flaws_and_growth:** Cornelius's need for order is both funny and a problem he must overcome. Zeph grows by becoming more confident. Show this through their actions.

**descriptor_cap:** No descriptor (adjective, phrase, or metaphor) may appear more than three times per manuscript.

### writing_limitations_and_goals

**avoid_clichés:** Avoid dark or gritty fantasy tropes. The villain is a criminal, not a monster.

**vivid_language:** Use strong, simple sensory words (crisp, dusty, silent, savory). Avoid describing light or air.

**emotional_clarity:** Slow down at key emotional moments to make sure the reader feels the connection between characters.

**internal_consistency:** Cornelius must always act like a dragon who loves order. His logic always serves his collection.

**show_don’t_tell:** Show affection through actions, like sharing food or protecting someone, rather than just saying it.

**strong_prose:** Keep the writing tight. Remove extra words that do not add meaning.

### scene_and_chapter_dynamics

**scene_opening:** Open every scene *in media res*—start in the middle of dialogue or action. Do not describe the sunlight, weather, or time of day. Establish the scene through immediate character interaction.

**scene_closure:** End on a clue, a choice, or a realization. Every scene should move the story or character arc forward.

**single_pov_per_scene:** One POV per scene/chapter (Cornelius).

**transition_handling:** Use clear breaks between scenes. Mark changes in location with immediate action in the new setting.

## cozy_fantasy_elements

**cozy_centrality:** The core appeal is **Comfort and Competence**. The fantasy elements exist to facilitate warmth, not terror. The reader should feel safe; even when the stakes are high (death/petrification), the narrative promises that order will be restored and the community will hold. Violence is low, blood is minimal, and the "Horror" (Gorgons) is revealed to be misunderstood hygiene/medicine.

**community_bonds_and_found_family:**
*   **The Library Trio:** Cornelius (The Grumpy Dad), Imelda (The Managing Mom), and Zeph (The Chaotic Son). They bicker, but they are fiercely protective of each other.
*   **The Ritual of Food:** Scenes often center around shared meals (The Harbor Fire Fish in Ch 6, Tea in Ch 7). This grounds the magic in domestic reality.
*   **The "Town" Character:** The town itself acts as a collective character. gossip travels faster than magic.

**cozy_tropes:**
*   **The Animal Companion:** Cornelius *is* the animal companion (to the town) and the protagonist. His "cat-like" behaviors (napping in sunbeams, knocking things over) provide levity.
*   **The Third Place:** The Library and Cassius's Bakery serve as safe havens where plot points are discussed over tea/bread.
*   **Domestic Magic:** Magic is used for chores (cooling boxes, mending pots, heating water) rather than war. This makes the world feel lived-in and functional.

**emotional_and_magical_risks:**
*   **Disruption of Peace:** The scary part isn't the "Monster," it's the *fear* the monster causes (The Mob, The Roundup). The risk is the breakdown of social trust.
*   **The Cost of Magic:** Magic is physical work. Using it too much (like Photine's healing) drains the user, making "Self-Care" a plot-critical mechanic.

**restorative_justice:**
*   **Healing > Punishment:** The goal of the mystery solution is not to put someone in prison, but to fix the root cause of the tragedy.
*   **The "Official Lie":** The series accepts that sometimes the Law (Theo) and Justice (Cornelius) diverge. The "Allergy" explanation in Ch 20 is a lie, but it restores the peace and saves a life, so it is the "Correct" Cozy ending.

**sensory_atmosphere:**
*   **Smell:** The strongest sense in the book (due to Cornelius/Zeph). Mint, Old Paper, Sun-baked Stone, Sea Salt, Sandalwood (The Clue), Lavender.
*   **Touch:** The texture of dragon scales, the rough edges of parchment, the coolness of the cisterns.
*   **Vibe:** "Sun-Drenched Melancholy." It is a bright, hot town with cool, dark shadows.


## world_building

**overview_setting_summary**: Solterra Bay is a vertical, sun-drenched coastal town defined by its "Cozy" magic, diverse integrated species (Humans, Myth-kin, Elves, Orcs), and a thriving tile industry. The setting mixes Antiquity and Early Medieval technology (oil lamps, aqueducts, no electricity), creating a world where ancient traditions and magical co-existence form the backdrop for community-driven mysteries.

### key_world_details
*   **Magic System:** Magic is "Cozy" and domestic—it alleviates suffering (sets bones, cools ice-boxes) but cannot reverse mortality (no resurrection) or instant-cure major wounds; it requires patient energy and rest.
*   **Demographics:** The town is a melting pot: Humans (40%), Myth-kin like Satyrs/Sirens/Gorgons (35%), Elves, and Orcs live together, each species filling ecological niches (e.g., Siren fishermen, Satyr bakers).
*   **Political Structure:** Local politics are driven by the feuds of the Three Tile Families (Kallistos, Doukas, Veneti), whose "Ceramic Wars" influence the economy more than the distant crown.
*   **Technology:** An Antiquity/Medieval blend featuring advanced aqueducts and bathhouses, centaur couriers for mail, and a reliance on wind/water power; no gunpowder or steam engines.
*   **Investigative Logic:** Mysteries are solved through a combination of "Dragon Forensics" (Cornelius's heightened senses/omniglot reading) and "Magical Trace Analysis" (Imelda's ability to sense magical residue).

### key_world_rules
*   **Rule of Conservation:** Healing magic draws from the patient’s biological reserves; a "quick heal" spell will leave the patient exhausted and ravenous.
*   **Rule of Species Logic:** Mysteries must account for biological traits—a Siren can hold their breath to escape via a cistern; a Satyr can jump vertiginous heights.
*   **Rule of Traces:** Every magical act leaves a signature (smell, residue, thermal shift) that can be tracked by a skilled practitioner or dragon.
*   **Rule of Bureaucratic Mercy:** The town prioritizes social stability over strict legalism; "Official Lies" (like the Allergy diagnosis) are accepted if they preserve the peace.
*   **Rule of Hospitality:** Sharing food (Ritual Meals like the "Harbor Fire") or vulnerability (Bathhouse deals) is the primary method of building trust.

## plot_development

### plot_beats

**beat_1_the_crime:** **Chapter 2 (The Statue).** The calm of the library is shattered when Imelda finds Brother Demos petrified in the archives. The "Ticking Clock" is established immediately: 72 hours before the stone becomes permanent.

**beat_2_the_sleuth_enters:** **Chapter 2/3 (The Assessment).** Cornelius takes the case, not out of altruism, but to protect his library from the "mess" of investigation. He immediately clashes with Captain Theo, establishing the "Law vs. Logic" dynamic.
