
**act_purpose:** Establish the "Status Quo" of **Camp Olea** (Cornelius trying to maintain Order vs. the Chaos of magic) and then break it with the Inciting Incident (Lukas's transformation). The act establishes the stakes (financial ruin vs. safety) and ends with the decision to hide the problem rather than call for help, launching the cover-up.

**major_act_reveals:**
*   Lukas transforms into a pigeon after eating "enhanced" Giggle-Glaze donuts.
*   The "Fun Magic" recipes are being hijacked by a mimic spell that twists the intent (Joy -> Flight).
*   Zeno is established as the prime suspect due to his chaotic nature and access to the kitchen, though evidence is circumstantial.
*   Maya transforms into a tortoise after eating a brownie "decorated" by Pippa (though Cornelius misses the direct link).
*   The Camp goes into lockdown ("The Souffle Protocol") to hide the transformations from the parents.

### tentpole_events

**tentpole_event_1:** **The Arrival & The Humiliation (Ch 1):** Cornelius arrives at the primitive campsite and attempts to assert dominance with his fire breath, but it fizzles into a pathetic puff, establishing his lack of biological superiority in this environment.

**tentpole_event_2:** **The Inciting Incident (Ch 2):** Lukas turns into a pigeon overnight after eating the modified Giggle-Glaze donuts, signalling that the magic has gone rogue.

**tentpole_event_3:** **The Lockdown (Ch 5):** After a second student (Maya) transforms into a tortoise, Cornelius attempts to arrest Zeno. Imelda intervenes, effectively sealing the camp ("The Souffle Protocol") and deputizing Zeno as a guard to keep him contained.

Zeno is caught nearby laughing about pranking the flour with yeast; Cornelius tests the flour but finds it only enhances levitation, not transmutation. Although Zeno is cleared of the specific mechanism, his confession to tampering makes him the prime suspect for the chaos. Cornelius hides the pigeon-Lukas in the dovecote, telling Imelda they are dealing with a mimic.

Later that night, screams from the Girls' Dorm reveal Maya's bed is empty, replaced by a Box Turtle hiding in her pillowcase; the brownie plate is empty. Cornelius spots Zeno lurking in the hallway and, recalling Zeno near the dessert tray, attempts to arrest him. Imelda intervenes, reframing the confinement as a "Special Night Watch Honor" which Zeno accepts enthusiastically. The camp is sealed, and Cornelius goes to sleep believing he has caught the culprit, unaware he just tucked the true source—Pippa—into bed.

### subplot_tracking

**subplot_development_1:** **Zeph's Struggle:** Zeph fails to connect with the human campers but discovers a talent for calming the transformed ones (The Pigeon), finding his niche as a "Herder."

**subplot_development_2:** **Zeno's Rebellion:** Zeno serves as the perfect Red Herring; his harmless pranks (dough tossing, yeast in flour) mask the serious magic, leading Cornelius to focus entirely on him.

**subplot_development_3:** **Pippa's Story:** Pippa is consistently shown reading fables and "helping" with sprinkles. Her narrative is happening in the background, visible to the reader but invisible to Cornelius.

### act_emotional_arc

**starting_emotion:** **Arrogant Superiority:** Cornelius believes he is biologically and intellectually superior to the chaotic "camp" environment.

**ending_emotion:** **Defensive Paranoia:** Cornelius realizes he is under siege by a magic he doesn't understand, but clings to a "False Certainty" that Zeno is the culprit to maintain his sanity.

**key_turning_points:**
1.  **The Failed Breath (Ch 1):** Cornelius's physical power is useless here.
2.  **The First Body Drop (Ch 2):** The situation escalates from annoyance to danger.
3.  **The "Arrest" (Ch 5):** Cornelius tries to use force/authority to solve the problem, only to be redirected by Imelda's soft power.
